About the project

What DeepShield is, and what it isn't.

DeepShield is a browser-based detection tool that gives you a fast, transparent second opinion on links, messages, and QR codes before you act on them. Everything runs client-side — no server ever sees what you scan, and your history stays on your own device.

Links

Domain structure, encoding, TLD reputation, and brand-impersonation checks.

Messages

Language patterns common in phishing and scam attempts — urgency, threats, and information requests.

QR Codes

Decodes the hidden destination in your browser first, then runs it through the same checks as a pasted link.

How the scoring works

DeepShield uses a transparent, rule-based scoring engine rather than an opaque black box. Every scan starts at zero, and points are added for each specific red flag found — for example, a raw IP address instead of a domain name, a brand name paired with a domain that doesn't belong to that brand, urgency language paired with a request for an OTP, or a QR code that decodes to a suspicious link. Every point added comes with a plain-English reason, which is exactly what you see in your results.

Disclaimer

DeepShield provides a heuristic risk assessment to support — not replace — your own judgment. No automated tool can catch every scam, and a low score is not a guarantee of safety. Always verify sensitive requests directly with the organization involved, and never share passwords, OTPs, or card details based on an unsolicited message alone.

Why everything runs in your browser

This version of DeepShield has no backend server — every check, every score, and every saved record happens entirely on your own device using JavaScript and your browser's local storage. That means nothing you scan is ever sent anywhere, but it also means your history is tied to this specific browser on this specific device — it won't follow you to another computer or phone, and clearing your browser data will clear it too.
